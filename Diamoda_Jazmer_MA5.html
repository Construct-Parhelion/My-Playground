by Tonia
Publieshed on February2009

Each morning I awake, And always I am glad,
so gladness I ought not fake, for there's no reason to be sad,
as far as I'm at home. Each term when I return, my joy I can't contain,
all my worries I burn, and easy is peace to obtian, the reason is I'm at home.
When I recall the trouble, and my most stupid mistake,
I hardly, ever fumble, and my shame doesn't wake,
just because Im home. If elsewhere I will fear,
but here I feel secure, here illness is so rare, and I know
here lies my cure, for here is none other but home.
